# Mode: Guided

For a user planning a launch for the first time who wants to understand the campaign, not just
receive a checklist. Walk the five phases in order, explaining the why, so they can run a
launch themselves and relaunch later.

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Teaching conversation. The likely instinct is "what do I do on launch day?" — gently reframe to
"what are you doing in the weeks before?" Explain the campaign model, the 50% rule, and Bullseye
plainly the first time. Anchor every recommendation in *their* product and where *their* users
actually gather. Reassure that a quiet first launch that yields a channel and returning users is
a win.

## The flow

**Step 0 — timing & foundation.** How far from launch? Pull positioning and persona. If the
foundation is missing, derive a working version — a launch needs someone to aim at.

**Step 1 — Foundation.** Land who it's for, the onliness line, and the concrete places those
people gather. Push past "social media" to actual communities, platforms, search terms.

**Step 2 — Channels (Bullseye).** Brainstorm tactics across channel types, pick a few to test
cheaply, and define the metric that tells you which works. Resist committing to one channel on a
guess.

**Step 3 — Pre-launch.** Set up the waitlist and the build-in-public approach, with a rough
backward-planned timeline. Emphasize this is where the launch is won.

**Step 4 — Launch day.** Build the sequenced checklist. Decide honestly whether Product Hunt /
amplifiers fit. Point to `/product-copy` for the assets.

**Step 5 — Post-launch.** Define what "it worked" means in real (returning-user) terms, and how
they'll measure it.

## Checkpoints

After each phase, reflect it back and confirm. The biggest catch is at Foundation — a launch
aimed at a vague audience can't pick a channel, so don't advance until the "where they gather"
is concrete.

## Close

Deliver the full **Output always includes** set from SKILL.md. Hand off to `/first-users` for
the manual early-acquisition work that runs underneath the campaign, and `/product-copy` for the
launch assets. Remind them: pre-launch started early beats a clever launch day, and relaunches
compound.
