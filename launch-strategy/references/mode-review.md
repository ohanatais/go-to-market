# Mode: Review

For a user who has a launch plan they want pressure-tested, or who has already launched and
wants an honest post-mortem. Diagnose before prescribing. The output is a clear read plus the
specific fixes (or the lesson and the next move).

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Honest and constructive. The most common problems are predictable: no pre-launch (a cold launch
day), no specific channel (spray and pray), and success measured in vanity (likes, a spike) not
traction (returning users). Name what's working, isolate the real gap, and tie every fix to the
campaign model. If they've already launched, separate "the launch underperformed" from "the
product or positioning is the real issue" — louder launches don't fix upstream problems.

## Reviewing a plan (pre-launch)

Check, in order:
1. **Foundation** — is the target and the where-they-gather concrete, or is it "everyone on
   social media"? Vague foundation is the root of most weak plans.
2. **Pre-launch** — is there any? A plan that's all launch-day and no warming is the number-one
   gap. If launch is close and there's no audience, say so and propose what to compress or
   whether to delay.
3. **Channels** — one guessed channel with everything poured in, or a cheap test of a few? Push
   toward Bullseye.
4. **Launch day** — sequenced, or a same-day scramble? Are amplifiers being defaulted to without
   a base?
5. **Metrics** — is "success" defined in returning-user terms before launch, or just "get
   attention"?

## Reviewing a launch (post-mortem)

1. **What actually happened** — separate vanity (spike, likes) from traction (sign-ups that came
   back). Be honest about which it was.
2. **Channel signal** — which channel, if any, produced returning users? That's the finding to
   build on, regardless of total volume.
3. **Diagnose a quiet launch** — was it under-warmed (pre-launch gap), aimed at the wrong place
   (channel/foundation), or is demand itself the question (upstream — positioning/product)? The
   fix differs completely, so name which it is.
4. **The next move** — optimize the working channel, fix the gap, and plan the *next* launch.
   Relaunches are the norm, not a consolation.

## Output

1. **Verdict** — one honest line (plan is launch-ready / has a critical gap; or the launch
   worked / was a vanity spike / revealed an upstream problem).
2. **What's working** — named and kept.
3. **Findings by impact** — pre-launch gap and foundation vagueness usually rank highest; then
   channels and metrics.
4. **Prioritized fixes or next moves** — ordered, concrete.
5. **Next step** — if the gap is upstream, route to `/positioning`; for the manual early users,
   `/first-users`; for assets, `/product-copy`. Plan the relaunch.
