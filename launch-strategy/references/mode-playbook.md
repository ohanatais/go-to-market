# Mode: Playbook

For a user who has the pieces (a foundation, a sense of channels) and wants the **concrete
launch plan and day-of checklist produced** — not a teaching walk. Decisive output, then
refine. Still derived from this product and market.

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Respect the upstream thinking. Pull positioning, persona, where-they-gather, and any existing
audience, and treat them as source. Produce a full launch plan in one pass — phased, sequenced,
with a real day-of checklist and real metrics — then refine where they push back. The
deliverable is something they could execute from.

## The flow (one pass, then refine)

1. **Confirm the foundation** in two lines: who it's for, the onliness line, and the specific
   places they gather. If any is vague, sharpen it — a launch aimed at "everyone" has no channel.
2. **Channel plan (Bullseye):** name the 2–3 channels worth testing for this product, the cheap
   test for each, and the metric that declares a winner. Don't hand them all 19; curate to the
   product and say why.
3. **Pre-launch plan:** the waitlist setup and the build-in-public approach, with a
   backward-planned timeline from launch day. Flag if they're starting too late and what to
   compress.
4. **Launch-day checklist:** sequenced, with rough timing — notify warm audience, publish
   assets, post to channels in order, engage, amplifiers (Product Hunt etc.) only if they fit.
5. **Metrics:** what "it worked" means in returning-user terms, and how they'll measure it.

Present it as one coherent plan with a clear timeline, so the user sees the campaign, not a
pile of tactics.

## Where to slow down

Even in playbook mode:
- **Where-they-gather must be concrete** — specific communities/platforms, not "social media."
- **Channels tie to a metric** — activity is not traction.
- **Amplifiers are a decision, not a default** — Product Hunt only if there's preparation and a
  base.

## Close

Deliver the full **Output always includes** set, write the plan to a file if useful, and hand
off to `/first-users` (the manual acquisition underneath the campaign) and `/product-copy` (the
launch assets). Note what's a hypothesis to validate, and remind them relaunches compound — this
is plan one, not the only shot.
