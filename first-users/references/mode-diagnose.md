# Mode: Diagnose

For a user who already has some early users (or some outreach happening) but it's not working —
nobody converts, or they sign up and vanish. The job: find where the leak is and fix the one that
matters most, before piling more people onto a broken funnel.

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Honest and specific. "I can't get traction" is a symptom with several very different causes, and
the fix depends entirely on which one. Resist the instinct to just "do more outreach" — if the
leak is activation or retention, more outreach pours water into a bucket with a hole. Localize the
leak first.

## Find the leak — walk the funnel in order

1. **Reach → reply.** Are outreach messages getting responses? If not, the problem is usually
   *targeting* (wrong/vague people) or a *pitchy* first message that triggers politeness or
   silence. Check the find list is named-specific and the opener learns rather than sells.
2. **Reply → try.** Do interested people actually try the product? If they stall here, it's often
   friction (the offer is too big a leap, sign-up is heavy) or the value isn't clear — and the
   fix may be a by-hand onboarding (do it *for* them) plus clearer copy (`/product-copy`).
3. **Try → stay.** Do people come back after the first session? If they sign up and vanish, this
   is the real problem and the most important one. The first session isn't delivering value fast
   enough, or the product doesn't yet solve the problem well enough. **Ask the churned users
   why** — early churned users will often tell you plainly.
4. **Stay → refer.** Do happy users bring others? If not, you may simply not be asking — or not
   asking for the *specific* introduction.

Find the *first* place the funnel leaks badly and focus there; fixing a later stage is pointless
if an earlier one is the real hole.

## The honest hard read

Sometimes the diagnosis is upstream: people understand the offer, try it, and leave because the
product doesn't yet solve the problem, or because the problem isn't painful enough for *these*
users. That's not an outreach fix — it's a product or positioning signal (`/positioning`,
`/personas`). Name it when that's what the evidence says; more outreach won't rescue a product
people don't want yet, and saying so honestly is more useful than a louder funnel.

## Output

1. **Verdict** — one honest line: where the funnel leaks (reach / try / stay / refer), or whether
   it's an upstream product/positioning signal.
2. **The evidence** — what in their numbers or stories points to that leak.
3. **The one fix that matters most** — concrete, at the leaking stage, not a list of everything.
4. **What not to do yet** — usually "don't scale outreach until the stay-rate is fixed."
5. **Next step** — the fix here, or route to `/product-copy` (clarity/friction), `/positioning` or
   `/personas` (upstream), or `/launch-strategy` once the funnel holds and a channel is ready to
   scale.
