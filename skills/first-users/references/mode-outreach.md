# Mode: Outreach

For a user who roughly knows who their users are and wants the **find list, outreach scripts, and
conversation plan produced** — ready to actually send. Decisive, concrete output, then refine.

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Respect that they want to *act*, not learn theory. Pull the persona and produce concrete,
ready-to-use material: a specific find list, an outreach opener and follow-ups, and the Mom-Test
questions — all in their language and tied to their real product. The deliverable is something
they can start sending today.

## The flow (one pass, then refine)

1. **Sharpen the target** in one line, then build the **find list**: specific people, communities,
   and recent problem-signals they can reach this week. If their targeting is still a demographic,
   convert it to named places and people before writing any script — a script into vagueness is
   wasted.
2. **Write the outreach opener** — references the specific signal, opens about *them*, makes clear
   it's not a pitch (because the first message learns). In the user's language and voice.
3. **Write the conversation questions** — Mom-Test style: about their life and past behavior, not
   your idea. Include the "soft offer" line for when the problem is confirmed real.
4. **Write the follow-ups** — for no-reply and for "sure, tell me more," kept short and human.
5. **Plan the by-hand onboarding** — what an overwhelmingly good first experience looks like for
   the ones who say yes, so outreach doesn't dead-end at a cold sign-up link.

Produce the scripts as ready-to-send text the user can copy, adapt, and fire — not templates full
of brackets they have to figure out.

## Where to slow down

Even fast:
- **Named people and places**, not demographics, or the outreach has nowhere to go.
- **Learn-not-pitch** framing in the first message — a pitch flips people into politeness and
  kills the signal.
- **The soft, last offer** — never lead with "want to buy/use my thing?"

## Close

Deliver the find list, the scripts, the questions, and the onboarding plan. Hand off to
`/product-copy` if the landing page or messages need real polish, and note that once a place
reliably produces returning users, it's a channel for `/launch-strategy` to scale.
